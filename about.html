About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Economics at HEC Montréal with extensive experience in applied microeconomics, labor economics, and development policy analysis.

I combine causal inference and eocnomic modeling to study the effects of economic policies and shocks.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Economics, HEC Montréal, Canada (2020–2026, expected)

    • Dissertation: “Stop That Train, I’m Starving: Impacts of Closing Rural Rail Stations.”
    Supervisors: Decio Coviello, Fabian Lange, and Ismael Mourifié.

  • M.Sc. in Statistics and Economics, ENSEA Abidjan, Côte d’Ivoire (2013–2016)

    • Focus on Econometrics and Applied Statistics.

Teaching Experience

  • Teaching Assistant – HEC Montréal

    • MATH80816: Applied Econometrics and Statistical Learning for Economics (Graduate Level), Summer 2023.

I led tutorials, graded assignments, and supported student learning in applied econometrics and causal inference methods.

Professional and Policy Experience

Before starting my Ph.D., I worked as an Economist Consultant at the World Bank, contributing to fiscal incidence, inequality, and social protection analyses across Sub-Saharan Africa and Central Asia.

  • Coauthored World Bank Policy Research Papers on Mali and Niger (2019).
  • Led microdata harmonization and fiscal microsimulation for multiple African countries.
  • Provided technical assistance and training for Ministries of Finance and Statistics Offices.
