Hello!

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Applied Economics at HEC Montréal. My research lies at the intersection of applied microeconomics, labor economics, development economics, and applied econometrics. I use causal and structural approaches to study how economic shocks and infrastructure policies shape welfare and labor outcomes in developing economies.

Job Market Paper

Stop That Train, I’m Starving: Impacts of Closing Rural Rail Stations

This paper studies the welfare impacts of the closure of rural railway stations in Côte d’Ivoire. Using household panel data and a difference-in-differences approach, I show that the closures led to a significant decline in consumption and a reallocation of labor away from nonfarm activities. I then estimate a Roy model of sectoral choice to quantify selection, returns, and welfare losses from reduced access to infrastructure.
